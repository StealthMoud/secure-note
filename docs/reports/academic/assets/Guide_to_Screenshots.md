# Step-by-Step Guide: Creating Your 15 Artifacts

Your professor requires **15 specific images** to be inserted into your report. This guide tells you exactly how to generate them using the data I provided in `docs/reports/academic/assets/Agile_Artifacts_Data.md`.

---

## Part 1: The Product Backlog (1 Image)
**Tool**: Notion
**Goal**: Create `Figure 1` for the Introduction.

1.  **Open Notion** and create a page named "SecureNote Product Backlog".
2.  Create a **Table Database**.
3.  **Columns**: Setup columns for "ID", "Category", "Item", "Points", "Priority", "Status".
4.  **Data**: Copy the rows from **Section 1 (Master Product Backlog)** of the Data file.
    *   *Tip*: You don't need all 120 items in one screenshot. Just capture the first 20-30 items to show the structure.
5.  **Action**: Take a screenshot.
6.  **File Name**: `master_backlog.png`.
7.  **Insert Location**: Replace the first placeholder in `Agile_Process.md`.

---

## Part 2: The Burndown Charts (14 Images)
**Tool**: Excel (or Google Sheets)
**Goal**: Create a chart for **EVERY** sprint.

**Repeat this process 14 times (once for each sprint):**

1.  **Open Excel**.
2.  **Get Data**: Go to **Section 2** of the Data file. look for "Sprint X Burndown Table".
3.  **Copy**: Copy the 3 columns (Day, Ideal, Actual).
4.  **Paste**: Paste into Excel.
5.  **Insert Chart**:
    *   Select the data.
    *   Insert > **Line Chart** (2D Line).
    *   **Title**: "Sprint X Burndown" (e.g., "Sprint 1 Burndown").
    *   **Style**: Make "Ideal" a dashed gray line and "Actual" a solid colored line.
6.  **Action**: Take a screenshot of the chart **only**.
7.  **File Name**: `sprint_X_burndown.png` (e.g., `sprint_1_burndown.png`).
8.  **Insert Location**: Go to `Agile_Process.md`, find "Sprint X", and replace the `PLACEHOLDER` at the bottom of that section.
